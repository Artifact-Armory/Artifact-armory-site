Cpt. White

British Parachute Regiment

Bio

Captain Edward White served with distinction in the British Parachute Regiment during the Second World War. Known for his strategic mind and courage during Operation Market Garden, Cpt. White led his men through intense combat in Arnhem.

Uniform & Equipment

White is shown wearing the iconic Denison smock, maroon beret, and standard webbing of British Airborne troops. His primary weapon is the Sten Mk V, along with entrenching tools and field gear carried for extended operations behind enemy lines.

Campaign

This miniature represents Cpt. White during Operation Market Garden, September 1944 – a bold Allied attempt to secure key bridges in the Netherlands. His loadout reflects late-war British airborne kit adapted for urban and riverine combat.
